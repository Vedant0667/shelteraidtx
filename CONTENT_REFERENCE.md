## HOW IT WORKS (4-Step Process)

1. **Collect Donations**
   - We collect new and gently used shoes from individuals, schools, and community organizations

2. **Sort & Organize**
   - Our volunteers sort donations by size, type, and condition to ensure quality

3. **Partner with Shelters**
   - We work directly with local shelters to understand their specific needs

4. **Make an Impact**
   - Shoes are distributed to those who need them most, providing comfort and dignity

## FAQ CONTENT

### Q: Are donations tax-deductible?
A: Yes! Shelter Aid TX is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. All donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. Our EIN is 93-3584886.

### Q: Where do the shoes go?
A: All donated shoes go directly to our shelter partners in the Dallas-Fort Worth area, including Family Gateway, Hope Restored Missions, AITF Services, Our Daily Bread, Journey to Dream, and Genesis Women's Shelter.

### Q: What types of shoes do you accept?
A: We accept all types of clean, gently used shoes in good condition - including athletic shoes, casual shoes, boots, and sandals. We welcome all sizes for men, women, and children.

### Q: How can I donate shoes?
A: You can drop off shoes at one of our partner locations, organize a shoe drive at your school or workplace, or request a volunteer pickup for donations of 30 or more pairs.

### Q: Can I make a monetary donation?
A: Yes! Monetary donations help us cover operational costs and expand our reach. All funds go directly to supporting our mission. You can donate through our secure payment system.

### Q: How can I volunteer or partner with you?
A: We're always looking for volunteers and partners! Fill out our contact form below with your interest, and we'll get back to you within 2 business days to discuss opportunities.

## BLOG POST

### "Starting a Nonprofit in High School: What's Actually Hard (and What Isn't)"
- **Author:** Vedant Subramanian
- **Date:** October 26, 2025
- **Category:** Building a Student Nonprofit
- **Series:** Post #1
- **Image:** /images/form-blog.jpg

**Content:**
When people hear I co-founded a nonprofit as a high schooler, they usually assume the hardest part was paperwork or legal stuff. But honestly? That was actually the easy part. The real challenges came from places I never expected.

**THE EASY PART: PAPERWORK**
Filing for 501(c)(3) status sounds intimidating, but it's mostly just filling out forms. Texas makes it pretty straightforward, and there are tons of free resources online. The IRS Form 1023-EZ took maybe a few hours, and we got our determination letter in about 3 weeks. Total cost: around $275 for filing fees.

**THE HARD PART: GETTING PEOPLE TO TAKE YOU SERIOUSLY**
This is what nobody tells you. When you're 16 and you email a shelter asking to partner with them, a lot of times you just... don't hear back. Or they assume you're doing this for college apps and won't stick around. It took months of consistent follow-up and actually showing up before people started taking us seriously.

**WHAT ACTUALLY WORKED**
- Starting small: Our first donation was 47 pairs of shoes. Not impressive, but it proved we could deliver.
- Being annoyingly consistent: Weekly emails, showing up at events, always following through.
- Letting the work speak: Once shelters saw we actually did what we said, doors opened.

**ADVICE FOR STUDENTS THINKING ABOUT THIS**
Don't wait until you have everything figured out. We started collecting shoes before we even had nonprofit status. The paperwork caught up eventually. What matters is that you start, and that you keep going when it gets frustrating.
